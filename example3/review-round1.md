## Barb (President)

**Round 1 Review – Headland-Buderim Croquet Club**

This is a strong first submission. The dark, institutional-minimal aesthetic you were after is mostly there. The grain, glassmorphic nav, lavender accents, and serious typography all feel premium and close to the Tenbin Labs reference.

**What works:**
- Overall mood and colour palette are excellent.
- Hero is dramatic and on-brand.
- Typography hierarchy is mostly good.
- The grain texture and glass buttons feel expensive.

**What is fundamentally wrong with the structure:**

The page flow is illogical and buries the most important information.

**Specific demands:**

1. **Move the Schedule section immediately after the Hero/Stats bar.**  
   This is the single most important piece of information for both members and prospects. It currently sits too far down.

2. **Merge About and Come-and-Try into one section.**  
   They are saying almost the same thing. The current split feels repetitive and wastes vertical space. Create a single “About & Welcome” section that covers the club’s character *and* the “first session free” offer.

3. **Reorder the remaining sections as:**
   - Hero + Stats
   - Schedule (new #2)
   - About & Welcome (merged)
   - Fees
   - Contact

4. **Remove the horizontal rules (`<hr class="divider">`) between every section.**  
   They break the premium flow and make the page feel like a 2008 blog. Use the existing padding and section labels instead.

5. **Fix the Come-and-Try / Contact image duplication.**  
   You are using the same dusk hero image in the contact section. Replace it with something else or remove the image from contact entirely and make that section text-only.

6. **Change the footer year.**  
   “© 2026” looks like a mistake.

**Mandatory structural change:**  
The current order (Hero → About → Schedule → Come-and-Try → Fees → Contact) is wrong. Schedule must move to position #2 and About/Come-and-Try must be merged.

Start over on the section order and merging. The current layout is fundamentally in the wrong sequence for a club that wants to attract new players.

**Score: 6/10**

Do the restructure above and come back for Round 2.

## Denise (New Member)

**Round 1 Review – Denise (4-month member, phone-first user)**

**Score: 6/10**

### What works well
- The dark, clean, premium “institutional minimalism” vibe is spot on. It feels serious and high-end, which matches the club’s competitive reputation.
- The hero image and overall atmosphere are beautiful.
- First session free is mentioned in the hero and repeated in the dedicated section — good.
- Phone number in the nav is correctly using `tel:` — thank you.

### What specifically needs changing (my demands)

1. **The hero does NOT answer my core question**  
   If I knew nothing about croquet, this site still doesn’t clearly tell me **how to turn up and play**.  
   “Start playing” and “First session free” are nice, but there’s no obvious “Just show up at these times” instruction right at the top. The call-to-action for new players is buried.

   **Demand:** Make the “first session is free + just turn up” message **impossible to miss** in the hero section on mobile. It must be visible without scrolling. Change the hero subheading or add a clear line like:  
   “**First session free. No booking needed. Just turn up any listed session.**”

2. **“Come and try” is buried**  
   Even though there’s a whole section, the nav CTA “Come and try” jumps to a section that appears after a lot of content. On mobile I have to scroll past stats, about, and the full schedule before I reach the new-player info.

   **Demand:** Move the Come and Try section **immediately after the hero/stats** (before About and Schedule). New players should not have to hunt for the most important information.

3. **Jargon problem**  
   The schedule uses “Association”, “Ricochet”, “Golf Croquet” with zero explanation. As a total beginner I have no idea what any of that means or which one I should turn up to.

   **Demand:** Add one simple beginner-friendly line at the top of the Schedule section:  
   “Don’t worry about the names — just pick any session time that suits you. All are welcome.”

4. **Mobile hero padding is too big**  
   On my phone the hero takes up way too much vertical space before I see anything useful. The stats bar is pushed far down.

   **Demand:** Reduce hero padding on mobile so the key message and CTAs appear higher on the first screen.

5. **“Come and try” button in nav should be more prominent**  
   The lavender/purple accent is used nicely, but the primary action for new players is still a bit timid.

   **Demand:** Make the “Come and try” button in the nav use the accent colour background (`background: var(--accent); color: #000;`) so it stands out more.

---

**Summary of mandatory changes for Round 2:**
- Hero must clearly say **how to turn up and play** without scrolling
- Move “Come and Try” section much higher (right after hero)
- Add beginner explanation to the schedule
- Tighten mobile hero spacing
- Make the nav “Come and try” button visually stronger

The design feels premium and serious — now it needs to be **actually welcoming and clear** to someone who knows nothing about croquet.

Ready for Round 2 when those changes are made.
