**Sandra’s Review (67, committee member – “I didn’t want a redesign, but here’s what still isn’t right”)**

**Overall rating: 5 / 10**

The site does hit the dark‑background, minimal‑look goal, but it leans a bit too far into a “creative‑agency” vibe rather than the sober, institutional feel of something like *tenbinlabs.xyz*. As someone who’s been on the committee for eleven years, I notice the little things that bother me even when everyone else seems happy.

---

### Specific demands (must be addressed before I’ll give it a higher score)

1. **Kill the grain‑texture overlay**     - The subtle noise pattern (`body::before`) adds visual clutter that feels more “hipster” than institutional. On a dark background it also reduces legibility of the white text, especially for older eyes. Remove it entirely (or at least make it optional via a user‑preference toggle).

2. **Tone down the accent colour**  
   - The current accent `--accent: #cecafb;` is a bright, pastel purple that pops too much against the black. For an institutional, sophisticated look we need a more restrained accent—think a muted slate‑blue or a soft cool‑gray (e.g., `#8a95b3` or `#a0a8c0`). Change the `--accent` token and update every place it’s used (buttons, links, hero‑label, etc.).

3. **Increase base body font size for readability**  
   - The body is set at `16px` with a mono font. While mono works for a technical feel, many members (myself included) find it harder to read at that size on a dark background. Raise the base to `18px` (or use a fluid clamp like `clamp(16px, 1.2vw, 20px)`) and keep the line‑height at `1.6` or slightly more.

4. **Add a skip‑to‑content link**  
   - For accessibility and to satisfy the institutional “no‑nonsense” expectation, provide a visually hidden skip link at the very top of the page (`<a href="#main-content" class="skip-link">Skip to main content</a>`) that becomes visible on focus. This helps keyboard‑only users jump straight to the primary content.

5. **Consistent heading hierarchy**  
   - The site uses `<h1>` only in the hero, then jumps to `<h2>` for section titles, which is fine, but the “section-label” paragraphs (`<p class="section-label">`) are styled like headings. Either turn those into proper `<h3>` elements (with appropriate styling) or remove the class and rely on spacing alone. This will improve SEO and screen‑reader navigation.

---

### What’s already working (so I don’t seem completely unreasonable)

- The dark background (`#000000`) and primary white text (`#ffffff`) are spot‑on for the brief.  
- The use of the **Syne** display font for headings gives a modern, institutional feel without being decorative.  
- The layout is responsive, with sensible breakpoints and flexible spacing (`clamp`, `grid`, `flex`).  
- All images have appropriate `alt` attributes, and the colour contrast of text against the black background meets WCAG AA.

If the grain overlay is removed, the accent colour is softened, and the base text size is bumped up (plus the skip link), I’d be willing to raise my score to a **7 / 10**. Anything less and I’ll keep asking for tweaks—sorry, but that’s just how I’ve always operated on this committee.
